About Me

I am Mohammed Musfique Ahmed Chowdhury, a graduate of Electrical and Electronic Engineering (EEE) from the Ahsanullah University of Science and Technology (AUST), Bangladesh. My academic and project experience has provided me with a strong foundation in embedded systems, image processing, renewable energy technologies, VLSI design and power engineering.

My undergraduate thesis focused on the design, development, and testing of a low-cost telescope under adverse environmental conditions, where I worked at the intersection of optical engineering, electronics, mechanical design, and digital image processing. This project strengthened my interest in sensing systems, computational imaging, and low-cost scientific instrumentation.

Beyond coursework and academic projects, I pursued hands-on training in industrial motor control systems, practical electronics, and technical project implementation. I have also gained experience in data visualization, simulation tools, control systems, and circuit design through various academic and professional activities.

I am particularly motivated by interdisciplinary problems that merge embedded intelligence with modern energy systems and sensing technologies. My broader research interests include embedded systems for imaging, renewable energy systems, battery and hybrid energy storage, smart grid technologies, and computational imaging.
